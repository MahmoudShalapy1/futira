Coins

Cryptocurrency has rapidly evolved from a niche technology to a mainstream financial asset, and crypto coins are at the heart of this revolution. With thousands of digital currencies available, understanding the basics of crypto coins, their significance, and their potential benefits can help you make informed decisions in the ever-changing world of digital finance.

Definition

Crypto coins, often referred to simply as "cryptocurrencies," are digital or virtual currencies that use cryptography for security. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments (fiat currencies), crypto coins operate on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology. Blockchain is a distributed ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers, ensuring transparency and security.

How work

Crypto coins function through a process called mining or staking, depending on the coin. Mining involves solving complex mathematical problems to validate transactions and create new coins. Staking, on the other hand, involves holding coins in a wallet to support network operations and earn rewards. Both methods ensure the integrity and security of the blockchain network..

Definition

tokens are units of value issued on a blockchain. Unlike traditional currency, tokens are not physical; they exist only in digital form and operate on decentralized networks. They represent various assets or utilities and can serve multiple functions depending on their design and purpose. Tokens are often created and managed through smart contracts, which handle the issuance, transfer, and redemption of tokens based on predefined rules.

How work

Token standards define the rules and protocols for creating and interacting with tokens on a blockchain. The most common standard is created and managed through smart Ethereum’s ERC-20, which outlines a set of rules that all ERC-20 tokens follow, ensuring compatibility with Ethereum-based applications. Other standards include through smart ERC-721 for non-fungible tokens (NFTs) and ERC-1155 for multi-token contracts.
